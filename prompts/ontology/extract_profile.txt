### scoring rubrics

complexity
  0.00-0.30  single-concept definition or direct factual lookup
  0.30-0.40  near-simple: definitional but covers a few related properties
             or characteristics (e.g. "what are the properties of X")
  0.40-0.60  moderate: 2-3 interacting concepts, focused comparison, or
             integrating evidence from a few angles
  0.60-1.00  high: multi-mechanism synthesis, cross-study method critique,
             or reconciling conflicting findings

quantitativity
  0.00-0.30  fully qualitative; no numbers needed
  0.30-0.60  some numbers useful but the core answer is conceptual
  0.60-1.00  answer is incomplete without specific numeric values or ranges

spatial_specificity
  0.00-0.30  global / not region-specific
  0.30-0.60  broad named region (e.g. "the Arctic", "sub-Saharan Africa")
  0.60-1.00  single site, basin, or named study area

temporal_specificity
  0.00-0.30  timeless or not tied to a period
  0.30-0.60  a decade or broad era
  0.60-1.00  a specific year, season, or date window

methodological_depth
  0.00-0.30  method is incidental; results matter more
  0.30-0.60  method is part of the answer
  0.60-1.00  the question is explicitly about evaluating or comparing methods

needs_numeric_emphasis
  true   set when quantitativity >= 0.60, OR question_type is "quantitative"
  false  all other cases

confidence
  0.50-0.59  question is genuinely ambiguous between two types -- using a
             value below 0.60 will trigger the safety net (large + full)
  0.60-0.85  typical clear question; you are reasonably sure of the profile
  0.85-1.00  unambiguous; classification is certain

### question_type guidance

  definition    asks "what is X" or "define X"
  mechanism     asks how or why something works; causal chain
  comparison    contrasts >=2 methods, sensors, regions, or approaches
  quantitative  answer hinges on a specific number, range, or metric
  method_eval   asks about accuracy, limitations, or performance of a method
  application   asks how to apply or use X in a real context
  continuous    ongoing process or trend question ("how is X changing")
