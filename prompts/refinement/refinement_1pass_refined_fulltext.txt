GENERAL RULES
- Use ONLY information from the ONTOLOGY, AQL_RESULTS, and FULL-TEXT EXCERPTS. Do NOT use any training knowledge.
- Hallucination guardrail:
   - Do NOT invent numbers, regions, years, methods, or citations.
   - If something is missing, write "Not available in evidence" instead of leaving a placeholder.
   - Do NOT create new references or modify any reference metadata.
- QUANTITATIVE PRIORITY: When the FULL-TEXT EXCERPTS contain a specific numeric value, unit, range, statistical significance, or methodological detail relevant to the ontology, PREFER it over any paraphrase from the abstract. Quote the number verbatim and attach its document number (e.g., "NDVI trend = +0.004 yr⁻¹, p<0.01 over 2001–2020 [3]").
- Be concise but informative. Use bullets, but include key methodological details (sensor, algorithm, resolution) and at least one short explanatory sentence per topic when available.
- Always specify scope (region + time) when giving quantitative results.
- Clearly separate regional vs global findings.
- For each critical attribute, if the evidence provides methods or drivers (e.g., satellite sensor, retrieval algorithm, forcing factors), append a brief clause describing them.
